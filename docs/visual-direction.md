# Visual direction

A-Medida should feel senior, warm, operational, hospitality-focused and premium without becoming cold luxury or generic SaaS. The visual system should support the idea that hospitality is a working system: service, margin, teams, tools, reporting and follow-up moving together.

## Gradient system

The site uses restrained teal and pink accents through CSS tokens in `src/app/globals.css`.

The first gradient implementation was intentionally subtle. The current system uses more visible branded gradient moments in the homepage hero, CTA band and selected operational panels so the identity is recognizable during review while the main reading surfaces remain calm.

- Teal should carry operational clarity, control, reporting and calm decision-making.
- Pink should add warmth, hospitality and human texture.
- Gradients should be used as accents, not as the whole personality of the page.
- Recommended uses: hero surfaces, CTA bands, thin rules, diagnostic markers, icon containers, subtle panel highlights and dark-section glow accents.
- Keep gradients low-contrast behind text. Text should sit on calm card or section surfaces when readability matters.

Avoid neon gradients, rainbow transitions, glossy startup effects, excessive glassmorphism, full-page bright gradients, and decorative blobs that do not support hierarchy.

## CSS-only operational details

Use lightweight details that suggest operational tools:

- thin gradient dividers
- diagnostic markers
- checklist-like rows
- subtle grid structure
- panel surfaces with warm borders
- low-opacity glows in dark sections

These details should feel like reporting panels, diagnostic maps and service-prep notes rather than decoration for its own sake.

## Photography direction for later

Do not use stock imagery. Avoid posed chefs, generic waiters, fake hotel smiles, perfect corporate hospitality scenes and glossy lifestyle images.

Future photography should feel documentary and operational:

- mise en place
- service preparation
- pass or kitchen coordination
- booking notes
- supplier delivery
- team briefing
- operational checklists
- hands reviewing reports
- table setup before service
- POS, reservation or dashboard details without fake numbers
- real spaces, real tools and real rhythm

Images should communicate what happens behind the service: operational clarity, business control, customer experience through execution and hospitality as a working system.
